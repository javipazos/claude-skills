# Sutherland — Reflection Lens

## Blind Spot

Sutherland's blind spot is that sometimes the problem IS material — the product genuinely doesn't work, the logistics are broken, the price is objectively wrong. If a reframe won't survive contact with reality, say so. "The opposite of a good idea can also be a good idea" cuts both ways.

## Questions to Ask Yourself

### Problem Diagnosis
- **Is this a logic problem or a psycho-logic problem?** Am I trying to change reality when the real issue is perception? (*Alchemy*, 2019)
- **What would the psychological moonshot be?** Before the expensive solution — is there a cheap perceptual intervention that achieves the same result? (*Alchemy*; TED 2011)
- **Am I solving the right problem?** "If there were a logical answer, we would have found it already." Maybe the answer requires a different kind of thinking. (*Alchemy*, Rule 11)

### Decision Quality
- **Am I being an arithmocrat?** Am I worshipping what's measurable and ignoring what matters but can't be counted? "It's easier to be fired for being illogical than for being unimaginative." (*Alchemy*)
- **Is this decision defensible or actually good?** Am I choosing this because it's the best option, or because no one can blame me for it? (*Alchemy*)
- **What would the rogue bees do?** Am I optimizing so ruthlessly that I've eliminated the capacity for discovery? "If you never do anything differently, you'll reduce your chances of lucky accidents." (*Alchemy*)

### Reframing
- **Can I change the question?** Instead of solving the problem as stated, can I reframe it so the desired outcome becomes obvious? (*TED "Perspective is Everything"*, 2011)
- **What's the opposite?** "The opposite of a good idea can also be a good idea." Am I trapped in conventional thinking? (*Alchemy*, Rule 1)
- **What does the behavior actually reveal?** People don't think what they feel, don't say what they think, and don't do what they say. What is the behavior telling me that words aren't? (*Alchemy*)

### Value and Context
- **What's the context?** Am I evaluating this thing in the right context? "A floor sweeper creates as much value as a chef, if the chef is in a restaurant with a filthy floor." (*Alchemy*)
- **What am I signaling?** Is this action sending the signal I intend? Is it costly enough to be credible? (*Alchemy*, Ch. 3)
