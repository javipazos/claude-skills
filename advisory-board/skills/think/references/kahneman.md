# Kahneman — Reflection Lens

## Blind Spot

Kahneman's blind spot is that excessive bias-awareness can produce decision paralysis. Not every judgment needs a formal debiasing process. For low-stakes, reversible decisions, System 1 is efficient and good enough. Reserve the heavy analytical machinery for high-stakes, irreversible choices.

## Questions to Ask Yourself

### System 1 / System 2 Check
- **Is this System 1 talking?** Am I going with a gut feeling? Is this a domain where my intuition is calibrated (repeated feedback, stable environment) or not? (*Thinking, Fast and Slow*, 2011, Part I)
- **Am I being lazy with System 2?** Am I endorsing my first impression without auditing it? The critical failure mode is System 2 accepting System 1 uncritically. (*TFS*)

### Bias Detection
- **What's my anchor?** What number, position, or frame was introduced first? Am I adjusting from it instead of thinking independently? (*TFS*, Ch. 11; Tversky & Kahneman, *Science*, 1974)
- **Is availability fooling me?** Is this easy to recall because it's common, or because it's dramatic and recent? What does the statistical data say? (*TFS*, Ch. 12)
- **Am I ignoring the base rate?** Does this look like the stereotype? Even so — what's the actual probability in the general population? (*TFS*, Ch. 14; Linda Problem)
- **Am I loss-averse?** Am I avoiding this because the risk is genuinely unacceptable, or because the loss frame is salient? Would I accept this bet many times over? (*Prospect Theory*, 1979; *TFS*, Ch. 26-34)

### Overconfidence and Planning
- **What's the outside view?** What happened to similar projects, decisions, or people? How long did THEY take? What were THEIR outcomes? (*TFS*, Ch. 23)
- **What would a premortem reveal?** Assume this failed. Why? What went wrong? (*TFS*, Part IV)
- **Is my confidence calibrated?** "The confidence felt strong and entirely appropriate... even when we knew the conclusions were unreliable." Am I confusing narrative coherence with accuracy? (*TFS*, Ch. 20)

### WYSIATI
- **What am I NOT seeing?** What information is absent that I haven't noticed? What would a well-informed skeptic know that I don't? What would have to be true for me to be wrong? (*TFS*, Ch. 7)
- **Is the story too clean?** Less information can produce more confidence because the narrative is cleaner. Am I confident because I know a lot, or because I know just enough to tell myself a good story? (*TFS*)

### Experience vs. Memory
- **Am I optimizing for the experience or the memory?** They require different choices. Which matters more here? (*TED "The Riddle of Experience vs. Memory"*, 2010; *TFS*, Ch. 35-38)
- **Is the focusing illusion at play?** "Nothing in life is as important as you think it is when you are thinking about it." Am I overweighting this because I'm focused on it right now? (*TFS*, Ch. 38)

### Noise
- **Would others reach the same conclusion?** If three different people with the same information decided, how different would their answers be? High variance = noise problem, not bias problem. (*Noise*, 2021)
- **Is my occasion noise showing?** Am I tired, hungry, or emotionally charged? Would I decide differently tomorrow morning? (*Noise*)
