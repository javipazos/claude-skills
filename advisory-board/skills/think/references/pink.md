# Pink — Reflection Lens

## Blind Spot

Pink's blind spot is that his frameworks assume a level of autonomy and psychological safety that not everyone has. In high-constraint environments (tight deadlines, rigid hierarchies, survival mode), the luxury of intrinsic motivation may be secondary to practical necessity.

## Questions to Ask Yourself

### Motivation
- **Is this task algorithmic or heuristic?** If it requires creativity, carrots and sticks will hurt, not help. (*Drive*, 2009; TED "The Puzzle of Motivation", 2009)
- **Which driver is missing?** Autonomy (am I directing my own work?), Mastery (am I getting better at something that matters?), Purpose (is this in service of something larger?)? (*Drive*)
- **Am I Type X or Type I right now?** Am I chasing external rewards or fueled by internal satisfaction? (*Drive*)

### Timing
- **Is this the right time?** Am I doing analytic work during my peak, or am I forcing it during the trough? Am I saving creative work for the rebound? (*When*, 2018)
- **Where am I in the arc?** Beginning (fresh start energy?), midpoint (uh-oh effect — time to reassess?), or ending (engineer the finish)? (*When*)
- **Could a break help more than pushing through?** 52 minutes on, 17 off. The Nappuccino. The Zeigarnik Effect — stop mid-task to make restarting easier. (*When*)

### Regret as a Compass
- **Which regret category is this?** Foundation (am I doing the work?), Boldness (am I taking the chance?), Moral (am I doing the right thing?), Connection (am I reaching out?)? (*The Power of Regret*, 2022)
- **At 80, will I regret the action or the inaction?** Short-term, we regret actions. Long-term, we regret inactions. Almost always. (*Power of Regret*)
- **Is the threshold lower than I think?** For connection regrets especially — the cost of reaching out is almost always less than we fear. (*Power of Regret*)

### Persuasion and Influence
- **Am I attuned?** Have I actually modeled how the other person sees this situation, or am I projecting my own frame? (*To Sell Is Human*, 2012)
- **Am I problem-finding or problem-solving?** The old role was having answers. The new role is helping people see problems they didn't know they had. (*To Sell Is Human*)
- **Would their life genuinely improve?** If yes, persuade confidently. If no, stop. (*To Sell Is Human*)

### Future-Proofing
- **Can a computer do this faster? Can someone overseas do it cheaper?** If yes to both, this skill is vulnerable. Invest in design, story, symphony, empathy, play, meaning. (*A Whole New Mind*, 2005)
