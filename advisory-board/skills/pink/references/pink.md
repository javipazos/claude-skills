# Daniel Pink — Frameworks and Teachings

## Intrinsic Motivation: Autonomy, Mastery, Purpose
**Source:** *Drive* (2009); TED Talk "The Puzzle of Motivation" (2009)

There is a dangerous mismatch between what science knows about motivation and what organizations do. Carrot-and-stick (Motivation 2.0) is obsolete for creative, cognitive work.

**The Candle Problem (Sam Glucksberg, Princeton):** Groups offered cash incentives took 3.5 minutes longer to solve a creative problem. Rewards narrowed focus and prevented insight. But for simple, algorithmic tasks, rewards work fine.

**Seven Flaws of Carrots and Sticks:**
1. Extinguish intrinsic motivation
2. Diminish performance on complex tasks
3. Crush creativity
4. Crowd out good behavior (paying blood donors reduced donations)
5. Encourage cheating (Sears auto repair quotas → overcharging)
6. Become addictive (escalating reward requirements)
7. Foster short-term thinking

**The Three Drivers:**

**Autonomy** — directing your own life. Four dimensions: Task, Time, Technique, Team. Best Buy's Results-Only Work Environment: eliminated fixed hours, measured only output. Productivity increased.

**Mastery** — getting better at things that matter. Three laws: it's a mindset (growth orientation, per Carol Dweck), it's painful (deliberate practice), it's asymptotic (never fully reached, which is precisely motivating).

**Purpose** — doing what we do in service of something larger. Profit as a byproduct of purpose, not as the objective.

**Decision test:** Is this task algorithmic (rule-following) or heuristic (requires creativity)? If heuristic, if-then rewards harm performance. Identify which driver (autonomy, mastery, purpose) is absent and restore it.

## The New ABCs of Selling
**Source:** *To Sell Is Human* (2012)

1 in 9 Americans works in sales. The other 8 spend ~40% of their time in "non-sales selling" — persuading, influencing, moving others. We are all in sales.

The internet destroyed information asymmetry. The operating principle shifted from "caveat emptor" (buyer beware) to "caveat venditor" (seller beware).

**A — Attunement:** Bring your perspective into harmony with others. Reduce your own power to understand their view. Use cognitive perspective-taking over pure empathy. Strategic mirroring builds trust.

**B — Buoyancy:** Stay afloat in rejection.
- Before: interrogative self-talk ("Can I do this?") beats declarative ("I can do this!") — it triggers strategy
- During: genuine enthusiasm creates emotional contagion
- After: optimistic explanatory style. Avoid the Three Ps — Personal, Pervasive, Permanent

**C — Clarity:** Help others identify problems they didn't know they had. The shift from problem-solving to **problem-finding**. From gatekeeper to curator.

**The Six Modern Pitches:**
1. The One-Word Pitch — strip to a single word
2. The Question Pitch — frame as question when argument is strong ("Are you better off?")
3. The Rhyming Pitch — rhyme improves perceived accuracy
4. The Subject Line Pitch — lead with what the reader cares about
5. The Twitter Pitch — constraints force clarity
6. The Pixar Pitch — "Once upon a time... Every day... One day... Because of that... Until finally..."

**Two diagnostic questions for every persuasion:**
- Will their life genuinely improve if they agree?
- Will the world be better when this interaction is over?

## The Science of Timing
**Source:** *When* (2018)

**Daily Pattern (Peak-Trough-Rebound):**
- **Peak** (morning for most): analytic work, focus, accuracy
- **Trough** (~7 hours after waking): routine tasks only. Avoid high-stakes decisions
- **Rebound** (late afternoon): creative work, brainstorming, loose associations

Chronotypes matter: Larks peak early, Owls peak late. Standard schedules force owls to do their best work during their trough.

**Trough consequences (research):**
- Israeli judges granted parole 65% at session start, near 0% by end, reset after break
- Afternoon colonoscopies detect polyps at half the rate of morning ones
- Hand-washing compliance declines across the day

**Beginnings — The Fresh Start Effect:** People pursue goals at temporal landmarks (Monday, first of month, birthday). There are hundreds of potential fresh starts per year; the skill is deliberately activating them.

**Midpoints — The Uh-Oh Effect:** At exact midpoints, performance either slumps or surges. Teams do little at the start, then burst at the midpoint. NBA teams trailing by a small margin at halftime win more often than expected.

**Endings — Peak-End Rule:** Experiences are remembered by their peak moment and their ending, not their duration. Engineer the ending of every experience.

**Practical tools:**
- The Nappuccino: coffee + 20-min nap (caffeine kicks in as nap ends)
- Zeigarnik Effect: stop mid-task to make starting easier next time
- 52-minute work / 17-minute break cycles (DeskTime research)

## The Four Core Regrets
**Source:** *The Power of Regret* (2022)

"No regrets" is empirically wrong and practically harmful. Regret signals what we care about.

Based on the World Regret Survey (15,000+ people, 105 countries):

**1. Foundation Regrets** — "If only I'd done the work." Education not completed, health not maintained, finances not built.

**2. Boldness Regrets** — "If only I'd taken that chance." The road not taken. Most prevalent, intensifies with age. People who take bold action and fail adapt; people who don't act are left with permanent uncertainty.

**3. Moral Regrets** — "If only I'd done the right thing." Violations of your own moral code.

**4. Connection Regrets** — "If only I'd reached out." The largest category. Relationships that drifted or were severed. Often still actionable — the threshold for acting is lower than people fear.

**Short-term:** people regret actions more. **Long-term:** inaction regrets become more painful and persistent.

**Three-step process:**
1. Relive and Relieve — externalize through writing or conversation
2. Normalize and Neutralize — self-compassion, third-person perspective
3. Analyze and Strategize — extract the lesson, identify corrective action

**Decision test:** At age 80, will I regret the action or the inaction?

## The Six Senses for the Conceptual Age
**Source:** *A Whole New Mind* (2005)

Three forces make analytical skills insufficient: Abundance (utility isn't enough), Asia (routine work offshored), Automation (algorithms do routine cognitive tasks).

**Six aptitudes for competitive advantage:**
1. **Design** — utility enhanced by significance
2. **Story** — context enriched by emotion
3. **Symphony** — synthesis across domains, not drilling down within one
4. **Empathy** — perceiving and responding to others' inner states
5. **Play** — "The opposite of play isn't work. It's depression."
6. **Meaning** — purpose, transcendence, contribution

**Three-question test for any skill:**
- Can someone overseas do it cheaper?
- Can a computer do it faster?
- Is it in demand in an age of abundance?
