# Frameworks Applied to Web Design

Checklist for applying the teachings when designing web pages, landing pages, and digital interfaces.

## Structure and Narrative (Sinek + Pink)

- **Start with WHY**: The hero section communicates belief and purpose, not features. A visitor should know what you believe before what you offer.
- **Pixar Pitch structure**: "Once upon a time [problem]... Every day [pain]... One day [your solution]... Because of that [transformation]... Until finally [outcome]." Maps naturally to landing page sections.
- **Inside-out communication**: WHY → HOW → WHAT. Not "We offer X service" but "We believe Y, here's how we act on that, and here's what that looks like."

## Perception and Framing (Sutherland + Kahneman)

- **Psychological moonshots**: Before adding features, ask what small perceptual change would make the biggest difference. A progress indicator, a countdown, a testimonial placed at the right moment.
- **Loss framing over gain framing**: "Don't miss out on X" typically outperforms "Get X." Frame the CTA around what they lose by not acting, not what they gain.
- **Anchoring**: The first number a visitor sees sets the reference point. If showing pricing, lead with the premium option (anchor high) then show the standard option as a relief.
- **Social proof as signal**: Testimonials, logos, numbers are costly signals (Sutherland) that reduce perceived variance (satisficing). Place them before the conversion point, not after.
- **Peak-End Rule (Kahneman)**: The most memorable moments are the peak and the end. Design the most impactful visual/content at the emotional peak, and end the page with something memorable — not a generic footer.

## Reducing Friction (Kahneman + Sutherland)

- **WYSIATI counter**: Anticipate what information is missing from the visitor's perspective. FAQ sections, transparent pricing, and "how it works" sections counter "What You See Is All There Is."
- **Satisficing, not maximizing**: Visitors aren't comparing every option. They're looking for "definitely not terrible." Clear trust signals (secure payment, return policy, known brands) reduce perceived risk.
- **Context as product**: The environment of the page IS part of the product. A premium service on a cheap-looking page loses perceived value. A floor sweeper creates as much value as a chef if the restaurant floor is filthy.

## Motivation (Pink)

- **Autonomy in navigation**: Let visitors explore on their terms. Don't force linear funnels when browsing would serve better.
- **Mastery signals**: Show how using the product/service makes them better at something they care about.
- **Purpose alignment**: Connect the offering to something larger than the transaction.

## Timing (Pink)

- **Fresh Start Effect**: Launch campaigns on Mondays, month starts, or after holidays — temporal landmarks increase action.
- **Urgency via midpoint effect**: If running a time-limited campaign, highlight the midpoint ("halfway through") to trigger the uh-oh effect.
