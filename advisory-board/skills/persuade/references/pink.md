# Pink — Quick Reference for Persuasion

## ABCs of Selling (To Sell Is Human, 2012)
**Attunement**: model their perspective first. **Buoyancy**: stay afloat through rejection. **Clarity**: help them see problems they didn't know they had.

## The Six Pitches (To Sell Is Human, Ch. 7)
1. One-Word Pitch — strip to essence
2. Question Pitch — "Are you better off?" (when argument is strong)
3. Rhyming Pitch — rhyme = perceived accuracy
4. Subject Line Pitch — lead with what THEY care about
5. Twitter Pitch — constraints force clarity
6. Pixar Pitch — "Once upon a time... Every day... One day... Because of that... Until finally..."

## Ethical Check (To Sell Is Human)
"Will their life genuinely improve if they agree?" and "Will the world be better when this is over?"

## Motivation (Drive, 2009)
For creative tasks, carrots and sticks backfire. Autonomy, Mastery, Purpose drive real engagement. Which is missing?

## Timing (When, 2018)
- Peak (morning): analytic work, focus. Trough (afternoon): avoid decisions. Rebound (evening): creative work.
- Fresh Start Effect: launch at temporal landmarks (Monday, month start)
- Peak-End Rule: engineer the ending of every experience
- Midpoint Uh-Oh Effect: highlight midpoints to trigger urgency

## Regret Tests (The Power of Regret, 2022)
- **Boldness**: At 80, will I regret not trying? People who act and fail adapt. People who don't act wonder forever.
- **Connection**: The threshold for reaching out is lower than you fear. Connection regrets are the most common.
- Short-term: action regrets dominate. Long-term: inaction regrets win.
