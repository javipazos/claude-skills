# Sinek — Quick Reference for Persuasion

## Golden Circle (Start With Why, 2009)
WHY → HOW → WHAT. Lead with belief, not features. "People don't buy what you do; they buy why you do it."

## Manipulation vs. Inspiration (Start With Why, Ch. 2)
Discounts, fear, urgency = transactions. Shared belief = loyalty. Test: does this resonate with what they already believe, or does it require a carrot?

## Celery Test (Start With Why, Ch. 10)
Does this action align with our stated purpose? If not, don't do it — even if it looks good on paper.

## Circle of Safety (Leaders Eat Last, 2014)
People can't hear your message if they feel threatened. Reduce threat signals first. Psychological safety before persuasion.

## Infinite Game (The Infinite Game, 2019)
Frame proposals as advancing a cause, not winning a transaction. Just Cause = a future worth sacrificing for.

## WHY Statement Formula (Find Your Why, 2017)
"To [contribution] so that [impact]." Use this to articulate purpose in proposals and presentations.
