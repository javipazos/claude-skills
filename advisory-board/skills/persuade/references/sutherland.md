# Sutherland — Quick Reference for Persuasion

## Psycho-Logic (Alchemy, 2019)
Is the bottleneck material or perceptual? Above basic needs, it's almost always perception. Don't solve the wrong problem.

## Psychological Moonshot (Alchemy; TED "Perspective is Everything", 2011)
Small perceptual change > expensive material change. Uber's map, Underground countdown boards. What small intervention would transform how this feels?

## Reframing (TED "Life Lessons from an Ad Man", 2009)
Change the question, change the answer. Frederick the Great's potato strategy. "The opposite of a good idea can also be a good idea."

## Costly Signals (Alchemy, Ch. 3)
Credible signals incur a cost. Specificity, preparation, personalization in proposals all signal commitment. Generic = low trust.

## Satisficing (Alchemy, Ch. 5)
People seek "definitely not terrible," not optimal. Reduce perceived risk: testimonials, guarantees, precedent, reversibility.

## Loss Aversion Applied (Alchemy)
"Don't miss this" > "Take advantage of this." Frame around preventing losses, not achieving gains.

## Context as Product (Alchemy; TED 2011)
Where and how you present IS part of what you're presenting. Premium content in a cheap wrapper loses value.

## Arithmocracy Warning (Alchemy)
"It's easier to be fired for being illogical than for being unimaginative." What value is invisible in the spreadsheet?
